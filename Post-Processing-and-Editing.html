POST PROCESSING AND EDITING

Post-processing and editing are essential steps in the photography workflow that allow photographers to enhance, refine, and transform their images after they are captured. It involves using software tools and techniques to make adjustments, corrections, and creative enhancements to the original photograph.

Exposure & Contrast

Adjusting the brightness, highlight, shadow, and general range of tones to get the look you want and make sure the picture is properly exposed.

Sharpening & Noise Reduction

Making the picture sharper so that features come out and getting rid of any noise or graininess that isn't wanted.

Retouch & Spot REmoval

Having rid of distractions or flaws, smoothing out skin flaws, or doing more thorough editing as needed.

Color & White Balance

Fine-tuning the color temperature, tint, saturation, and hue to create an emotion or fix any color casts.

Cropping & Straightening

Changing the layout by cutting the picture or fixing any tilted borders to make the whole thing look better and have more balance.

Creative Enhancement

Using creative filters, adding scenes, changing the color curves, or adding special effects to create a certain artistic style or mood.

RULE OF THIRD

Make a 3x3 grid with the frame and put important things along the lines or at the points where the lines meet. This method gives the piece more balance and visual interest.

LEADING LINE

 Use lines like roads, paths, or building features to lead the viewer's eyes to the main subject or focus point of the picture. Leading lines add depth and give the impression that something is moving.

BALANCE & SYMMETRY

 You can create a sense of unity by putting things in an orderly way or by balancing the way things or colours are placed in the frame.

FRAMING

 Use archways or tree branches or other natural or man-made things to frame the main subject. This method makes the subject stand out and gives the picture more depth
